The Challenge

Data was fragmented across transactional systems, marketing platforms, fulfillment tools, and legacy reporting databases. Business teams relied on analysts for routine reports, creating bottlenecks and inconsistent metrics definitions. Executive leadership lacked timely, trusted visibility into performance across channels and regions.

The client required an enterprise-grade data foundation paired with business intelligence capabilities that could scale with transaction volume and support both governed executive reporting and self-service analytics.

Our Approach

Deep Hill designed and implemented an end-to-end data platform and BI solution, establishing a single source of truth for key business domains while enabling faster, more reliable decision-making across the organization.

  • Cloud-native data lakehouse architecture with governed ingestion pipelines
  • Dimensional models for sales, customer, marketing, and operations analytics
  • Executive dashboards with standardized KPI definitions
  • Self-service BI layer for business analysts and department leads
  • Data quality monitoring, lineage, and access control frameworks

Estimated Benefits

Based on post-implementation analysis and client feedback, Deep Hill estimates the following percentage improvements:

55% Faster executive reporting turnaround
48% Improvement in data pipeline reliability
42% Increase in self-service analytics adoption
35% Reduction in time-to-insight for business teams

Estimated benefits are based on observed project outcomes and client-reported improvements. Results vary by organization, scope, and operating environment. No financial figures are quoted.

Outcome

The data platform and BI implementation gave the client a durable analytics foundation aligned with growth ambitions. Leadership gained faster, more consistent visibility into performance, while business teams reduced dependency on manual reporting and began making decisions from shared, trusted data.
