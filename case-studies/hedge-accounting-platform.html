The Challenge

The bank's hedge accounting processes were spread across legacy systems, spreadsheets, and manual reconciliations. Complex instrument types, multi-currency positions, and evolving accounting standards created bottlenecks at month-end. Teams spent disproportionate time validating data rather than analyzing portfolio risk and effectiveness.

Leadership required a single platform capable of supporting the full trade lifecycle — from capture and valuation through hedge designation, effectiveness testing, journal generation, and external reporting — without compromising auditability.

Our Approach

Deep Hill partnered with the bank's finance, risk, and technology teams to design and deliver an end-to-end hedge accounting platform. We mapped existing workflows, defined a target architecture, and implemented the solution in phased releases to minimize disruption to live trading operations.

  • Unified trade capture and instrument master data across asset classes
  • Automated hedge designation and effectiveness testing workflows
  • Integrated general ledger posting and reconciliation engine
  • Configurable reporting layer for internal and regulatory outputs
  • Full audit trail with role-based access and approval controls

Estimated Benefits

Based on post-implementation analysis and client feedback, Deep Hill estimates the following percentage improvements:

45% Faster month-end hedge reporting cycles
55% Reduction in manual reconciliation effort
38% Improvement in audit readiness consistency
30% Fewer reporting exceptions per quarter

Estimated benefits are based on observed project outcomes and client-reported improvements. Results vary by organization, scope, and operating environment. No financial figures are quoted.

Outcome

The platform gave the bank a scalable foundation for hedge accounting across regions and product lines. Finance teams shifted from reactive firefighting to proactive portfolio oversight, while technology teams gained a maintainable, extensible system aligned with enterprise standards.
